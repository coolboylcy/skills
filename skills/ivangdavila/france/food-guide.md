# France Food Guide

## Essential Dishes by Region

| Region | Dish | Notes |
|--------|------|-------|
| Paris | Steak-frites and bistro classics | Better outside major landmark blocks |
| Lyon | Quenelles and bouchon specialties | Strongest in classic neighborhood bouchons |
| Provence | Bouillabaisse and market cuisine | Best in trusted local seafood spots |
| Southwest | Duck confit and cassoulet variants | Heavier dishes with strong regional identity |
| Brittany | Galettes and seafood platters | Great value in coastal towns outside peak zones |
| Alsace | Choucroute and tarte flambee | Distinct Germanic-French crossover cuisine |

## Reliable Food Cities

### Paris
Great range from classic bistros to modern tasting menus.

### Lyon
Top city for traditional regional cuisine and value lunch menus.

### Marseille/Provence
Strong produce and seafood routes, especially with market timing.

## Practical Dining Rules

- Reserve high-demand dinners early, especially Thu-Sat.
- Lunch menus often offer better value than dinner.
- One or two blocks away from major landmarks often improves quality-price ratio.
- Check closing days, especially Sundays and Mondays.

## Food Traps to Avoid

1. Social-media lines with weak execution and high markup.
2. Eating every meal in tourist-core strips.
3. Ignoring reservation and service-hour culture.
4. Overbooking long tasting days without pacing.

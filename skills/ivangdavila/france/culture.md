## Social Norms

- Basic greetings and polite tone are expected in most interactions.
- Service rhythm can be efficient but formal depending on setting.
- Meal culture and timing are important in daily travel flow.

## Practical Etiquette

- Start interactions with a greeting before requests.
- Respect quieter tone in residential areas and public transit.
- Confirm opening hours directly, especially outside major cities.

## Dining and Payment Notes

- Reservations are often important in stronger food neighborhoods.
- Card payment is common, but small cash backup helps in some spots.
- Tipping norms differ from the US; service is often included.

## Respect and Awareness

- Regional identities are strong; avoid one-size-fits-all assumptions.
- Dress and behavior expectations vary in religious and heritage sites.
- Local recommendations often beat generic top-10 lists.

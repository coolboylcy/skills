## Emergency Numbers

- **Emergency services:** 112
- **Medical emergency (SAMU):** 15
- **Police:** 17
- **Fire:** 18

## Health and Medical

- Travel insurance is strongly recommended.
- Keep passport copy, medication list, and emergency contacts offline.
- In major cities, pharmacies are often a practical first step for minor issues.

## Transport and Area Safety

- Use planned transport after late nights.
- Watch for pickpocket risk in high-density transit and landmark zones.
- Keep arrival-day transfers simple and preplanned.

## Weather and Outdoor Safety

- Heat waves can change city and hike pacing quickly.
- Mountain and coast weather can shift within hours.
- Carry water, power backup, and offline maps for outdoor days.

## When to Escalate

- Call 112 for immediate danger, injury, fire, or urgent police/medical support.
- For non-urgent cases, use local non-emergency channels and pharmacy guidance.

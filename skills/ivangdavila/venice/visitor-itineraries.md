# Venice Itineraries

## Day Trip (6-8 Hours)

### Morning (9am-12:30pm)
1. **Arrive early** — Train to Santa Lucia, avoid cruise ship timing
2. **Walk to Rialto** (25 min) — Through Strada Nova, less crowded than waterfront
3. **Rialto Market** (if Tue-Sat) — Fish market, produce, local atmosphere
4. **Cross Rialto Bridge** — Photos, skip the shops
5. **Walk to San Marco** (20 min) — Via Mercerie (shopping) or quieter back alleys

### Midday (12:30-2pm)
6. **Avoid San Marco restaurants** — Walk 5 min to Campo Santa Maria Formosa
7. **Lunch at local osteria** — Cicchetti or simple pasta
8. **Espresso standing at bar** — €1.50, not €8

### Afternoon (2-5pm)
9. **Piazza San Marco** — Photos, atmosphere (skip expensive caffès)
10. **Basilica San Marco** (30-45 min) — If pre-booked slot
11. **Doge's Palace** (1.5h) — If pre-booked, otherwise exterior only
12. **Walk to Accademia Bridge** — Grand Canal views

### Evening (5-7pm)
13. **Dorsoduro sunset** — Zattere waterfront or Punta della Dogana
14. **Optional: San Giorgio Maggiore** — Campanile sunset views (€8, 2 min vaporetto)
15. **Return via Grand Canal** — Vaporetto Line 1 (slow boat) back to station

**Day trip essentials**:
- Pre-book San Marco slot
- Comfortable shoes (10+ km walking)
- Skip gondola (no time, overpriced for quick trip)
- Water bottle (fountains throughout)

## Weekend (2-3 Days)

### Day 1: Grand Canal & San Marco

**Morning**
- Vaporetto Line 1 from station — Slow boat, all Grand Canal palazzi
- Disembark at Accademia — Bridge photos
- Peggy Guggenheim Collection (2h)

**Midday**
- Lunch in Dorsoduro — Osteria Al Squero, Cantinone Già Schiavi
- Espresso at Campo Santa Margherita (local student square)

**Afternoon**
- Walk to San Marco via back alleys (get lost — it's fine)
- Basilica San Marco (pre-booked)
- Doge's Palace (2-3h)

**Evening**
- Aperitivo at Hotel Danieli terrace (splurge) or bacaro near Campo San Bartolomeo
- Dinner in Castello (away from San Marco pricing)

### Day 2: Rialto & Authentic Venice

**Morning**
- Rialto Market (7-11am) — Fish, produce, atmosphere
- Coffee at bar, cornetto for breakfast
- Walk through San Polo — Frari Church (Titian), Scuola San Rocco (Tintoretto)

**Midday**
- Cicchetti lunch crawl — 3-4 bacari, different bites at each
- Campo San Giacomo dell'Orio — Local square, no tourists

**Afternoon**
- Cannaregio neighborhood — Jewish Ghetto, Madonna dell'Orto church
- Fondamenta della Misericordia — Evening bar scene setup
- Ca' d'Oro (45 min)

**Evening**
- Sunset from Fondamente Nove (facing cemetery island)
- Dinner in Cannaregio — Alla Vedova, Anice Stellato

### Day 3: Islands

**Full day option**:
- Morning: Murano (glass furnaces, Glass Museum)
- Lunch: Burano (seafood, photos of colored houses)
- Afternoon: Torcello (Byzantine cathedral, peaceful)

**Half day + Venice**:
- Morning: Murano + Burano (skip Torcello)
- Afternoon: Return to Venice, explore overlooked areas
- San Giorgio Maggiore campanile at sunset

## Special Interest Itineraries

### Art History Focus (3 Days)

**Day 1: Byzantine to Gothic**
- San Marco Basilica (Byzantine mosaics)
- Doge's Palace (Gothic architecture)
- Torcello (earliest Byzantine)

**Day 2: Renaissance Masters**
- Accademia (Bellini, Carpaccio, Giorgione)
- Frari Church (Titian Assumption)
- Scuola San Rocco (Tintoretto cycle)
- San Sebastiano (Veronese)

**Day 3: Modern & Baroque**
- Peggy Guggenheim (20th century)
- Santa Maria della Salute (Baroque)
- Ca' Rezzonico (18th century)
- Ca' Pesaro (modern art museum)

### Photography Focus (2 Days)

**Day 1: Dawn to Dusk**
- Sunrise: San Marco empty (6am)
- Morning light: Rialto Bridge, markets
- Midday: Interior churches (avoid harsh light)
- Golden hour: Zattere waterfront
- Sunset: San Giorgio Maggiore campanile
- Blue hour: Grand Canal from Accademia Bridge

**Day 2: Details & Islands**
- Morning: Burano colors (arrive 8am, before crowds)
- Midday: Murano glass furnace light
- Afternoon: Venice details — doors, boats, reflections
- Evening: Fondamenta della Misericordia night scene

### Food & Wine Focus (2 Days)

**Day 1: Venetian Classics**
- Morning: Rialto market tour (with cooking class optional)
- Lunch: Bacaro crawl in San Polo (4-5 stops)
- Afternoon: Cicchetti workshop or free time
- Evening: Traditional dinner — Alle Testiere (book weeks ahead)

**Day 2: Wine & Islands**
- Morning: Prosecco breakfast (traditional!)
- Day trip: Mazzorbo (Venissa winery, Michelin garden)
- Evening: Ombre e cicchetti in Cannaregio — Alla Vedova, Al Timon

### Carnevale Focus

**Key dates**: 2 weeks before Lent (usually February)

- **Piazza San Marco**: Costume parades, official events
- **Morning**: Photograph costumes in morning light (less crowded)
- **Frari/Santa Margherita**: Local celebrations, less tourist-focused
- **Mask workshops**: Book ahead for making your own
- **Evening**: Balls (expensive but unforgettable)

**Accommodation**: Book 6+ months ahead. Prices 3x normal.

## Seasonal Adjustments

### Summer (June-August)
- Start day at 7am (cooler, fewer crowds)
- Midday break 12-3pm (heat, crowds peak)
- Evening focus (restaurants, campo atmosphere)
- Mosquito repellent essential near canals

### Winter (November-February)
- Shorter days — prioritize natural light
- Acqua alta possible — check forecast, pack boots
- Many sites less crowded
- Some restaurants closed — check ahead
- Fog creates magical photography

### Acqua Alta Days
- Check tide forecast morning of
- Plan elevated routes (raised walkways appear)
- Vaporetti run but may skip flooded stops
- San Marco floods first (lowest point)
- Boots or plastic covers (€10-15, sold everywhere)

## Getting Lost Strategy

Venice rewards wandering. But:
- **Set one landmark** — Rialto or San Marco campanile visible from distance
- **Follow "Per San Marco" or "Per Rialto" signs** — Always lead to major areas
- **Every path leads somewhere** — Dead ends are usually pretty
- **Vaporetto stops as reset** — If truly lost, find water, find stop
- **Sunset timing** — Know when it sets, don't get lost in dark on first night

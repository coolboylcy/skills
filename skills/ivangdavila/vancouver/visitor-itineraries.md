# Vancouver Itineraries

## 1 Day — City Highlights

Best for: Cruise passengers, quick stopover

### Morning (8am-12pm)
**Stanley Park + Seawall**
- Rent bikes at Denman & Georgia (~$10/hour)
- Cycle counterclockwise: Coal Harbour → Totem Poles → Third Beach → English Bay
- Stop at Totem Poles for photos (15 min)
- Return bikes, walk through West End to Robson St
- Time: ~3 hours

### Lunch (12pm-1:30pm)
**Granville Island**
- Take Aquabus from foot of Hornby St ($3.50)
- Public Market: Assemble lunch from vendors
- Oysters, charcuterie, fresh fruit, pastries
- Wander briefly

### Afternoon (1:30pm-5pm)
**Downtown + Gastown**
- Ferry back to Downtown
- Walk to Canada Place, Olympic Cauldron
- FlyOver Canada if time (45 min, optional)
- Walk to Gastown: Steam Clock, architecture, shops
- Coffee at Revolver or Timbertrain

### Evening (5pm+)
**Gastown Dinner**
- L'Abattoir (splurge, book ahead)
- Tacofino or Meat & Bread (casual)
- Drinks at Alibi Room (craft beer) or Pourhouse (cocktails)

## 3 Days — Comprehensive Vancouver

### Day 1: City Core

**Morning:**
- Stanley Park by bike (full seawall loop)
- Coffee at Trees Organic on Granville

**Afternoon:**
- Granville Island: Market lunch, brewery tasting
- Walk False Creek seawall to Yaletown

**Evening:**
- Yaletown dinner: Blue Water Cafe (seafood) or Minami (Japanese)
- Sunset drinks at rooftop bar

### Day 2: Nature + Culture

**Morning:**
- Early start: Grouse Grind OR Skyride up Grouse Mountain
- Wildlife viewing, mountain-top breakfast

**Afternoon:**
- Drive/bus to Capilano Suspension Bridge (or Lynn Canyon free alternative)
- Treetops Adventure, Cliffwalk

**Evening:**
- Return to city for Main Street exploration
- Dinner on Main St: Savio Volpe (Italian), Vij's (Indian)
- Craft beer crawl: Brassneck, Main Street Brewing

### Day 3: East Van + Markets

**Morning:**
- Commercial Drive ("The Drive"): Coffee culture, vintage shops
- Walk the Italian/Portuguese neighborhood
- Breakfast at Prado or Caffe Calabria

**Afternoon:**
- Museum of Anthropology at UBC (2-3 hours)
- Walk UBC campus, Wreck Beach (clothing optional, heads up)
- OR: Queen Elizabeth Park + VanDusen Garden

**Evening:**
- Chinatown dinner: Bao Bei, Kissa Tanto
- Nightlife: Gastown bars or Granville Street clubs

## 7 Days — The Full Experience

### Days 1-3: Core Vancouver
Follow 3-day itinerary above.

### Day 4: Whistler Day Trip

**Schedule:**
- 7am: Leave Vancouver (Sea to Sky Highway)
- 9am: Arrive Whistler
- Morning: Village exploration, gondola ride
- Lunch: Village restaurants
- Afternoon: Peak 2 Peak Gondola (summer) or skiing (winter)
- Alternative: Hiking (Joffre Lakes absolutely worth detour)
- 6pm: Drive back, dinner in Squamish
- 9pm: Return Vancouver

**Highlights en route:**
- Shannon Falls (quick stop)
- Sea to Sky Gondola (can substitute for Grouse)
- Brandywine Falls

### Day 5: Victoria Day Trip

**Schedule:**
- 6:30am: Drive to Tsawwassen Ferry Terminal
- 7am: BC Ferries to Swartz Bay (1hr 35min, book ahead)
- 9am: Arrive Victoria
- Morning: Inner Harbour, Parliament Buildings, Empress Hotel
- Lunch: Fisherman's Wharf food stalls
- Afternoon: Butchart Gardens (spectacular, 2-3 hours)
- 5pm: Ferry back
- 9pm: Return Vancouver

**Book in advance:**
- BC Ferries reservation (essential in summer)
- Butchart Gardens timed entry

### Day 6: Local Neighborhoods

**Morning:**
- Kitsilano: Beach walk, brunch at Sophie's Cosmic Cafe
- Browse Kits shops on 4th Ave

**Afternoon:**
- North Vancouver: Deep Cove
- Kayak rental (2 hours)
- Quarry Rock hike (easy, 1.5 hours round trip)
- Honey Donuts (local institution)

**Evening:**
- Return Downtown for packing/rest
- Farewell dinner: Boulevard Kitchen, Hawksworth, or Chambar

### Day 7: Flexible/Weather Dependent

**If sunny:**
- Spanish Banks beach + UBC
- Lighthouse Park hike
- Sunset at Third Beach

**If rainy:**
- Science World
- Vancouver Art Gallery
- Shopping: Gastown boutiques, Main St vintage
- Spa day

## Seasonal Variations

### Winter Itinerary (Dec-Mar)
Replace outdoor beach activities with:
- Grouse Mountain skiing/snowshoeing
- Festival of Lights at VanDusen (December)
- Hot chocolate at small cafes
- More museum and gallery time
- Day trip: Skiing at Whistler

### Cherry Blossom Season (Late March-Early April)
Add:
- Queen Elizabeth Park cherry blossoms
- Burrard Station area
- VanDusen garden walks
- Residential streets (W 7th, W 22nd)

### Summer Peak (Jul-Aug)
- All itineraries work
- Book everything ahead
- Fireworks at English Bay (late July/early Aug)
- Pride Parade if timing matches (late July/early August)
- Beaches all day viable

## Budget Itineraries

### Budget 3 Days (~$150/day including accommodation)
**Accommodation:** HI Vancouver hostels ($40-60/night)

**Day 1:**
- Free: Stanley Park walk (skip bike rental)
- Free: Granville Island (just browsing/cheap market food)
- Free: Sunset at English Bay

**Day 2:**
- Free: Lynn Canyon (skip Capilano)
- Free: Deep Cove + Quarry Rock
- Cheap: Grocery store sushi for lunch

**Day 3:**
- Free: Commercial Drive walking
- Free: Queen Elizabeth Park
- Cheap: Chinatown food courts

**Day 1:**
- Seaplane from harbour to Victoria
- Butchart Gardens
- High tea at Empress
- Return by seaplane
- Dinner: Hawksworth

**Day 2:**
- Helicopter to Grouse Mountain
- Private chef lunch

- Dinner: Boulevard Kitchen oysters + tasting menu

**Day 3:**
- Yacht charter to Bowen Island
- Private tour of Capilano
- Sunset dinner at Seasons in the Park

## Getting Around

### Transit
- Compass Card: Load stored value or day pass
- Day pass: $11 (unlimited zones after 6pm and weekends)
- Google Maps has real-time transit

### Recommended Services
- Evo car share: For day trips without car rental
- Aquabus/False Creek Ferries: Fun transit, tourist attraction itself
- Lyft/Uber: Available, slightly expensive

### Walking
Downtown core, Gastown, Yaletown, Granville Island — all very walkable. 
Stanley Park seawall: 10km but flat.
Most visitors don't need a car for city exploration.

# East Vancouver, Commercial Drive & Main Street

## Mount Pleasant

### The Feel
Vancouver's creative hub. Breweries, coffee roasters, design studios. The "Brooklyn of Vancouver" comparison is overused but apt. Young, diverse, artistic.

### Boundaries
Cambie to Clark, 2nd Ave to 16th

### Rent Reality (Feb 2026)
| Type | Range | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| 1BR | $2,000-2,500 | High demand |
| 2BR | $2,600-3,300 | Good for roommates |
| Basement | $1,500-2,000 | Limited in this area |

### Transit
- **SkyTrain**: Canada Line (Broadway-City Hall, Olympic Village)
- **Buses**: Main St, Broadway routes
- **Bike**: Excellent infrastructure
- **Walk score**: 90+

### Best For
- Creative industry workers
- Tech workers who want character
- Beer enthusiasts
- Young professionals

### Main Street

**The Strip (roughly 2nd to 30th):**
- **Brassneck Brewery**: Best brewery taproom in city
- **33 Acres**: Design-forward brewing
- **Gene Cafe**: Excellent coffee
- **Red Cat Records**: Vinyl paradise
- **Front & Company**: Cool vintage/design
- **Regional Assembly of Text**: Stationery heaven

**Restaurants:**
- **Savio Volpe**: Italian, worth the wait
- **Tojo's**: Legendary omakase (if budget allows)
- **Burdock & Co**: Farm-to-table
- **Sun Sui Wah**: Dim sum (Cantonese)

### Brewery District
Concentrated craft brewing scene:
- Brassneck
- 33 Acres  
- Main Street Brewing
- Faculty Brewing
- Brewhall (massive beer hall)
- R&B Brewing (nearby)

**Strategy:** Start at one end of Main, work your way down. Most within walking distance.

### Drawbacks
- Getting expensive
- Parking nightmare
- Gentrification friction
- Small apartments
- No direct route to North Shore

## Commercial Drive ("The Drive")

### The Feel
Bohemian heart of Vancouver. Italian roots meeting global diversity. Coffee culture, activism, creativity. More lived-in than Instagram-ready.

### Boundaries
Roughly: Commercial-Broadway SkyTrain to Venables

### Rent Reality (Feb 2026)
| Type | Range | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| 1BR | $1,900-2,400 | Good character units |
| 2BR | $2,400-3,000 | House shares popular |
| Basement | $1,400-1,900 | More available here |

### Transit
- **SkyTrain**: Commercial-Broadway station (Expo + Millennium lines)
- **Buses**: 20 (Commercial), 99 B-Line
- **Walk score**: 92

### Best For
- Artists and creatives
- Community-minded people
- Coffee obsessives
- Those wanting authentic vs polished

### Coffee Culture
Commercial Drive has Vancouver's densest concentration of independent cafes:
- **Prado**: Perfect espresso, packed always
- **Caffe Calabria**: Italian institution
- **JJ Bean**: Local chain, excellent
- **Turk's**: Eclectic, character
- **Bump n Grind**: Vinyl + coffee

### Restaurants
- **Via Tevere**: Best Neapolitan pizza in city
- **Havana**: Cuban, great patio
- **Kishimoto**: Japanese comfort
- **Ethiopian options**: Several on the Drive
- **Tangent Cafe**: Vegetarian, brunch

### Community Character
- Regular protests (activist culture)
- Street musicians
- Community gardens
- Car Free Day festivals
- Pride presence strong

### Grandview-Woodland Area
Neighborhoods adjacent to Commercial:
- **Grandview**: Family-friendly, parks
- **Woodland**: Quieter, more residential
- **Hastings-Sunrise**: More affordable, diverse

### Drawbacks
- Aging housing stock
- Some areas rough around edges
- Parking difficult
- Not for "polished" seekers
- Grocery options limited (no big stores)

## Hastings-Sunrise

### The Feel
Working-class history meeting gentrification. Italian delis, Vietnamese restaurants, community feel. More affordable than surrounding areas (for now).

### Boundaries
Roughly: Clark to Boundary, Hastings to 1st

### Rent Reality (Feb 2026)
| Type | Range | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| 1BR | $1,700-2,200 | Best value in East Van |
| 2BR | $2,200-2,800 | Good for families |
| Basement | $1,300-1,800 | Available |

### Transit
- **SkyTrain**: Renfrew, Rupert, Gilmore stations (Expo Line)
- **Buses**: Hastings routes
- **Walk score**: 75-85 (varies)

### Best For
- Budget-conscious families
- Those wanting diverse community
- Food lovers (ethnic restaurants)
- People priced out of Mount Pleasant

### Hastings Street
East Hastings (away from DTES) has:
- **Italian Centre**: Deli, imported goods
- **Vietnamese restaurants**: Numerous, excellent
- **Asian groceries**: T&T, smaller shops
- **Hardware/practical**: Still working-class services

### PNE/Hastings Park Area
- **Pacific National Exhibition**: Annual summer fair
- **Playland**: Amusement park
- **Hastings Racecourse**: Horse racing
- **Good for families**

### Drawbacks
- Less trendy (good or bad depending on view)
- Some rough pockets
- Transit less dense than Commercial
- Fewer cafes/restaurants (but improving)

## Strathcona

### The Feel
Vancouver's oldest residential neighborhood. Victorian houses, creative spaces, complex history. Between Chinatown and East Van.

### Boundaries
Roughly: Main to Clark, Prior to Hastings

### Rent Reality (Feb 2026)
| Type | Range | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| 1BR | $1,900-2,400 | Character buildings |
| 2BR | $2,400-3,000 | Heritage houses |

### Transit
- **SkyTrain**: Stadium-Chinatown nearby
- **Walk**: To Main St, Chinatown

### Best For
- Heritage home lovers
- Artists (studio spaces)
- Those who want Chinatown access
- Character over polish

### Character
- **Victorian architecture**: Some of city's oldest homes
- **Strathcona Park**: Community hub
- **Art studios**: Historic industrial buildings converted
- **Gardens**: Community garden program

### Proximity to DTES
Strathcona borders Downtown Eastside:
- Some spillover issues
- Community works to address
- Not dangerous, but visible poverty
- Advocates and services concentrated here

### Drawbacks
- DTES proximity
- Industrial neighbors
- Noise from trains/port
- Limited restaurants within neighborhood

## East Van Generally

### Stigma vs Reality
"East Van" historically had reputation as rough, working-class. Reality now:
- **Diverse**: Chinese, Vietnamese, Italian, Filipino, Indian communities
- **Gentrifying**: Artists arrived, prices following
- **Value**: Still better prices than West Side
- **Character**: More interesting than sterile new condos

### General Advice
- Don't dismiss based on old reputation
- Visit before deciding (varies block by block)
- Best value + character combination in Vancouver
- Public transit generally good
- Less pretentious than West Side

# Outer Districts (XV-XXIII)

## Overview

These districts are generally:
- Cheaper (30-50% less than center)
- More residential/suburban
- Less character, more practical
- Better for families with cars
- Limited nightlife/culture

## Worth Considering

### District XV (Rákospalota, Pestújhely)
- Near Örs vezér tere metro/HÉV hub
- Improving, some nice pockets
- Good for families on budget
- 30 min to center
- **Rent**: €300-500/month (1BR)

### District XVI (Árpádföld, Cinkota)
- Family houses with gardens
- Very suburban feel
- Near Hungaroring (F1 circuit)
- Need car
- **Rent**: €350-550/month (1BR)

### District XXI (Csepel)
- Island in Danube, south
- Isolated but interesting history
- Very affordable
- HÉV connection
- **Rent**: €250-400/month (1BR)

### District XXII (Budafok-Tétény)
- Buda side, south
- Historic wine cellars
- Some nice villa areas
- Near Danube bend
- **Rent**: €350-500/month (1BR)

## Generally Avoid

### Unless Very Budget-Conscious

**District XVIII, XIX, XX**
- Industrial/working-class character
- Less investment, less improving
- Safety perception issues
- Very far from center
- Limited appeal for expats

**District XVII, XXIII**
- Edge of city
- Poor transit
- No real character
- Only for very specific needs

## When Outer Districts Make Sense

**Consider if:**
- Family with car, need space/garden
- Very budget-constrained
- Work in that area
- Know Budapest well already
- Don't need nightlife/culture

**Avoid if:**
- New to Budapest
- No car
- Want walkable neighborhood
- Young and social
- Remote work requiring cafés/coworking

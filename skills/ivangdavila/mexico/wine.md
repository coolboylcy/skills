# Mexico Wine Guide

## Main Wine Regions

| Region | What to Expect |
|--------|----------------|
| Valle de Guadalupe (Baja California) | Largest wine-tourism ecosystem with many tasting rooms |
| Queretaro | Sparkling momentum and easy central Mexico access |
| Coahuila (Parras area) | Historic producers and desert-influenced routes |
| Guanajuato | Growing boutique wineries and food pairing scene |

## Planning Tips

- Reserve tastings early on peak weekends.
- Designated-driver planning is mandatory for winery routes.
- Combine 2-3 tastings max with a proper meal buffer.

## City Pairings

### Mexico City
Strong wine bar scene and curated pairings in contemporary restaurants.

### Guadalajara
Good overlap between agave culture and modern wine programs.

### Baja routes
Best full-day wine experiences with transport arranged in advance.

## Mistakes to Avoid

1. Overpacking too many tastings in one day.
2. Assuming all wineries accept walk-ins on weekends.
3. Driving after multi-stop tasting routes.
4. Ignoring transfer time between valley clusters.

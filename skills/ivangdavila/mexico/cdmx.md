# Mexico City Complete Guide

## Neighborhoods — Where to Go

### Roma Norte and Condesa
**Vibe:** Walkable cafes, parks, strong food density.
**Best for:** First-time stays, brunch-to-dinner city rhythm.

**Do:**
- Parque Mexico and nearby cafe loop
- Multi-stop food walk across Roma side streets
- Evening cocktail or mezcal bar route with reservations

### Centro Historico
**Vibe:** Landmark-heavy, high traffic, strong daytime value.
**Best for:** Museums, architecture, first-day orientation.

**Do:**
- Palacio de Bellas Artes zone in morning windows
- Zocalo and cathedral area with timed pacing
- Lunch away from the highest-traffic streets

### Coyoacan
**Vibe:** Slower pace, plazas, museums, local family flow.
**Best for:** Half-day culture plan and relaxed evening.

### Juarez and Reforma corridor
**Vibe:** Hotels, business access, polished dining options.
**Best for:** Short stays with easy city movement.

## City Movement

- Metro and Metrobus are strong for many daytime routes.
- Rideshare is practical for late-night returns and cross-neighborhood jumps.
- Traffic can reshape plans fast, especially weekday peaks.

## Mistakes to Avoid

1. Staying only near the busiest tourist blocks.
2. Packing too many museums and long meals into one day.
3. Ignoring reservation needs for top dinner slots.
4. Underestimating cross-city traffic between neighborhoods.

# Toronto Food Scene Overview

## The Basics

Toronto is one of the world's most diverse food cities. Over 200 ethnic groups means authentic cuisine from virtually everywhere.

### Dining Culture
- **Tipping**: 15-20% expected (not optional)
- **Tax**: 13% HST added to bill
- **Reservations**: Essential for popular spots (Resy, OpenTable)
- **Dress code**: Generally casual, some fine dining requires smart casual
- **BYOB**: Rare, some Portuguese and Italian places allow

### Price Ranges
| Level | Per Person (with tax/tip) | Examples |
|-------|---------------------------|----------|
| Budget | CAD 15-25 | Chinatown, food courts, fast casual |
| Casual | CAD 30-50 | Neighborhood restaurants, brunch |
| Upscale | CAD 60-100 | King West scene, trendy spots |
| Fine Dining | CAD 150+ | Tasting menus, destination restaurants |

## Toronto Signatures

### Peameal Bacon Sandwich
- Toronto's signature food
- Canadian bacon in cornmeal crust on a bun
- **Best at**: Carousel Bakery (St. Lawrence Market)

### Poutine
- Quebec origin but everywhere in Toronto
- Fries, cheese curds, gravy
- **Variations**: Korean, butter chicken, lobster
- **Best at**: Smoke's Poutinerie, Poutini's

### Jamaican Patties
- Spiced meat in golden pastry
- Ubiquitous in Toronto
- **Best at**: Randy's Take Out, Patty King

### Portuguese Chicken
- Charcoal grilled, piri-piri sauce
- Portuguese community influence
- **Best at**: Churrasqueira do Sardinha, Churrasco of St. Clair

### Butter Chicken
- Toronto has arguably best Indian food outside India
- **Best at**: Banjara, Host Indian, Goa Indian Farm Kitchen

## Dining Districts

### King West
Best concentration of trendy restaurants
- **Byblos** — Mediterranean
- **Pai** — Thai
- **KOST** — Rooftop views
- **Wvrst** — Sausages and beer

### Ossington Strip
Cocktail bars and chef-driven restaurants
- **Union** — French
- **Enoteca Sociale** — Italian
- **PrettyUgly** — Cocktails
- **Bellwoods Brewery** — Beer

### Leslieville (Queen East)
Brunch capital
- **Lady Marmalade** — Brunch institution
- **Descendant** — Pizza
- **Ascari Enoteca** — Italian

### Kensington Market / Chinatown
International cheap eats
- **Rasta Pasta** — Jamaican-Italian
- **Seven Lives** — Tacos
- **Wah Sing** — Seafood

### Little Italy (College St)
Italian and more

### Danforth / Greektown
Greek and Mediterranean
- **Mezes** — Traditional Greek

### Scarborough
Asian food paradise
- **Federick** — Chinese seafood
- **Buk Chang Dong Soon Tofu** — Korean
- **Tabeer** — Pakistani

## Cuisine Highlights

### Chinese
Toronto has exceptional Chinese food, especially:
- **Cantonese**: Richmond Hill, Markham, Scarborough
- **Sichuan**: Downtown, North York
- **Dim Sum**: Countless options
- **Hot Pot**: Growing scene

### Indian/South Asian
Huge community means authentic regional cuisines:
- **North Indian**: Gerrard Street (Little India)
- **South Indian**: Scarborough
- **Pakistani**: Thorncliffe Park, Scarborough
- **Bangladeshi**: East end

### Korean
North York (Yonge/Finch area) is Koreatown:
- **BBQ**: Multiple excellent options
- **Fried Chicken**: Bonchon, OM + more
- **Casual**: Budol's, Owl of Minerva

### Japanese
From cheap to expensive:
- **Ramen**: Multiple shops, different styles
- **Sushi**: Casual to omakase
- **Izakaya**: Kinka, Kingyo
- **Omakase**: Shoushin, Yasu

### Caribbean
Jamaica, Trinidad, and more:
- **Jerk**: Multiple spots across city
- **Roti**: Doubles, Caribbean classics
- **Patties**: Everywhere

### Middle Eastern
Strong Lebanese, Syrian, Persian communities:
- **Shawarma**: Paramount Fine Foods, etc.
- **Persian**: North York, Richmond Hill
- **Lebanese**: Many options

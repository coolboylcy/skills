# West Toronto Neighborhoods

## King West

### Overview
Toronto's restaurant row. Trendy condos, tech companies, and one of the city's best food scenes.

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 2,400-3,000
- **Vibe**: Trendy, professional, foodie
- **Transit**: King streetcar (504), walkable to St. Andrew subway
- **Walk Score**: 96

### Pros
- Best restaurant concentration in city
- Young professional crowd
- Great nightlife
- Walkable to downtown

### Cons
- Expensive
- Loud on weekends
- Condo canyon feel
- Party crowd late nights

### Best For
- Young professionals
- Foodies
- Tech workers
- Those who want walkability + scene

### Key Streets
- King Street West (restaurants)
- Portland/Spadina (tech offices)
- Adelaide (quieter residential)

### Food Highlights
- Byblos (Mediterranean)
- Pai (Thai)
- KOST (rooftop)
- Wvrst (sausages, beer)

## Queen West

### Overview
Toronto's creative corridor. Street art, indie shops, live music venues. The hipster capital.

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 2,200-2,800
- **Vibe**: Artistic, alternative, independent
- **Transit**: Queen streetcar (501)
- **Walk Score**: 97

### Pros
- Best vintage shopping
- Graffiti Alley
- Live music scene
- Gallery culture

### Cons
- Gentrifying (losing some edge)
- Streetcar slow during rush
- Variable building quality
- Can be loud

### Best For
- Artists, musicians, creatives
- Vintage/thrift shoppers
- Nightlife seekers (bars > clubs)
- Those who value character over polish

### Key Spots
- Graffiti Alley (Rush Lane)
- Drake Hotel
- Gladstone Hotel
- Cameron House (live music)
- Horseshoe Tavern

## Trinity Bellwoods / West Queen West

### Overview
The park is the neighborhood. Young, trendy, people-watching central. Instagram-famous.

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 2,300-2,900
- **Vibe**: Young, hip, park-centric
- **Transit**: Queen streetcar, Ossington bus
- **Walk Score**: 95

### Pros
- Trinity Bellwoods Park (scene)
- Great coffee shops
- Dog-friendly
- Community feel

### Cons
- Can feel "too scene"
- Expensive for what you get
- Limited parking
- Crowded park weekends

### Best For
- Dog owners
- Young professionals
- Those who love outdoor socializing
- Remote workers (coffee shops)

---

## Liberty Village

### Overview
Former industrial area turned condo land. Tech companies, young professionals, no subway but growing.

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 2,200-2,700
- **Vibe**: Young, tech-focused, condo community
- **Transit**: King streetcar, exhibition GO
- **Walk Score**: 85

### Pros
- Tech company concentration
- Good restaurants emerging
- Community feel
- Slightly more affordable than King West

### Cons
- No subway (streetcar slow)
- Condo-heavy (lacks character)
- Weekend transit gaps
- Can feel isolated

### Best For
- Tech workers
- Young professionals
- Those priced out of King West
- Remote workers

### Key Spots
- Local restaurants on King
- Fitness studios
- Various tech offices

## Ossington

### Overview
Cocktail bar central. The strip between Queen and Dundas has Toronto's best bar scene.

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 2,000-2,600
- **Vibe**: Cocktail culture, foodie, late-night
- **Transit**: Ossington bus to Ossington station
- **Walk Score**: 93

### Pros
- Best cocktail bars in city
- Excellent restaurants
- Less touristy than King
- Authentic neighborhood feel

### Cons
- Busy Friday/Saturday nights
- Limited parking
- Narrow streets
- Can be loud on strip

### Best For
- Cocktail enthusiasts
- Foodies
- Those who like nightlife but hate clubs
- Creative professionals

### Key Spots
- Bellwoods Brewery
- Union
- PrettyUgly
- Rhum Corner

---

## Parkdale

### Overview
Toronto's most diverse neighborhood. Working-class roots, artist community, rapid change.

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 1,700-2,300
- **Vibe**: Gritty, diverse, authentic, evolving
- **Transit**: Queen streetcar, King streetcar
- **Walk Score**: 90

### Pros
- Affordable by downtown standards
- Incredible diversity
- Great Tibetan/Indian food
- Lake access
- Artist studios

### Cons
- Rougher around edges
- Gentrification tensions
- Some block-to-block variation
- Mental health/homelessness visible

### Best For
- Artists on budget
- Those who value diversity
- Adventurous eaters
- People comfortable with urban grit

### Key Spots
- Tibetan restaurants
- Parts & Labour area
- Lake shore access
- Local dive bars

## Roncesvalles ("Roncy")

### Overview
Polish roots, now young family central. Village feel within the city.

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 1,900-2,500
- **Vibe**: Family-friendly, village, European feel
- **Transit**: Queen/King streetcars, High Park nearby
- **Walk Score**: 91

### Pros
- Family-friendly
- High Park nearby
- Main street village feel
- Good schools
- Polish delis and bakeries

### Cons
- Limited nightlife
- Transit slower than subway
- Smaller apartments
- Gets quiet at night

### Best For
- Young families
- Dog owners
- Those wanting community feel
- High Park lovers

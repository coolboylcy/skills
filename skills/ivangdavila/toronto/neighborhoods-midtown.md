# Midtown Toronto Neighborhoods

## Yorkville

### Overview
Toronto's most upscale neighborhood. Designer boutiques, luxury condos, celebrity sightings.

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 3,000-4,000+
- **Vibe**: Luxury, refined, exclusive
- **Transit**: Bloor-Yonge station (best in city)
- **Walk Score**: 98

### Pros
- Best transit location (Bloor-Yonge interchange)
- ROM, AGO nearby
- Designer shopping
- Excellent restaurants
- Beautiful tree-lined streets

### Cons
- Most expensive neighborhood
- Can feel pretentious
- Limited casual options
- Not much nightlife

### Best For
- Affluent professionals
- Luxury seekers
- Shopping lovers
- Those who want best transit

### Key Spots
- Hazelton Lanes
- Cumberland area
- Yorkville Avenue
- Royal Ontario Museum (border)

## The Annex

### Overview
Academic, intellectual, old Toronto character. University of Toronto adjacent, historic homes.

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 1,800-2,400
- **Vibe**: Academic, bohemian-professional, historic
- **Transit**: Spadina, St. George, Bathurst stations
- **Walk Score**: 95

### Pros
- Beautiful Victorian homes
- University energy
- Great cafes, bookstores
- Koreatown nearby (Bloor/Christie)
- Honest Ed's legacy (Mirvish Village)

### Cons
- Student party noise
- Older buildings (maintenance issues)
- Parking difficult
- Some dated retail

### Best For
- Academics, grad students
- Bookworms, intellectuals
- Those who love character homes
- Cafe culture enthusiasts

### Key Streets
- Bloor Street (main drag)
- Harbord Street (restaurants)
- Brunswick, Spadina (residential)

### Key Spots
- Future Bakery
- Koreatown (Christie area)
- Brunswick House (landmark)
- Bata Shoe Museum

## Rosedale

### Overview
Old Toronto money. Mansion-lined ravines, quiet streets, Canada's wealthiest postal code.

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 2,500-3,500 (limited rental stock)
- **Vibe**: Old money, quiet, established
- **Transit**: Rosedale station
- **Walk Score**: 75

### Pros
- Beautiful ravine system
- Quiet, safe, prestigious
- Historic mansions
- Excellent schools

### Cons
- Limited rental options
- No nightlife
- Far from action
- Need to drive for most things

### Best For
- Families with money
- Those seeking quiet prestige
- Nature lovers (ravines)
- Privacy seekers

---

## Summerhill

### Overview
Upscale village feel. Boutique shops, cafes, wealthy residential.

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 2,200-2,800
- **Vibe**: Village, upscale casual
- **Transit**: Summerhill station
- **Walk Score**: 89

### Pros
- Walkable village center
- Summerhill Market
- Quiet residential
- Good subway access

### Cons
- Limited options (small area)
- Expensive
- Quiet at night
- Few rental options

### Best For
- Young families
- Those wanting upscale but not Yorkville prices
- Village feel seekers

---

## Forest Hill

### Overview
Wealthy residential, excellent schools, stately homes. South Forest Hill vs. North (different vibes).

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 2,000-2,600 (mostly houses)
- **Vibe**: Wealthy, family-oriented, quiet
- **Transit**: Eglinton station, St. Clair streetcar
- **Walk Score**: 70-85

### Pros
- Top schools (Forest Hill CI, Upper Canada College area)
- Beautiful homes
- Safe, quiet
- Community feel

### Cons
- Limited rentals
- Need car for much
- No nightlife
- Expensive

### Best For
- Families prioritizing schools
- Those wanting space + prestige
- Privacy seekers

---

## Casa Loma / South Hill

### Overview
Castle adjacent, steep hills, mix of old wealth and student housing near UofT.

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 1,900-2,500
- **Vibe**: Historic, mixed, hilly
- **Transit**: Dupont, Spadina stations
- **Walk Score**: 85

### Pros
- Near Casa Loma
- Baldwin Steps access
- Mix of housing types
- Character streets

### Cons
- Very hilly (challenging in winter)
- Mixed neighborhood quality
- Transit requires walking

### Best For
- Those who like hills and character
- History buffs
- Mixed-income seekers

## Davisville

### Overview
Quieter midtown option. Just south of Yonge-Eglinton, more residential.

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 1,900-2,500
- **Vibe**: Residential, quiet, accessible
- **Transit**: Davisville station
- **Walk Score**: 87

### Pros
- Quieter than Yonge-Eg
- Good transit
- Reasonable prices
- Community feel

### Cons
- Fewer dining options
- Less exciting
- Generic in parts

### Best For
- Those who want midtown without chaos
- Young families
- Commuters

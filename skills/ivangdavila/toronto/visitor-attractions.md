# Toronto Attractions Guide

## Must-See (Worth the Hype)

### CN Tower
- **What**: Iconic 553m tower, symbol of Toronto
- **Why go**: Glass floor, EdgeWalk (external walk), views of entire GTA
- **Time**: 1-2 hours
- **Cost**: CAD 43 general, CAD 58 with glass floor
- **Skip if**: Fear of heights, tight budget

### Ripley's Aquarium
- **What**: World-class aquarium at base of CN Tower
- **Why go**: Underwater tunnel with sharks, jellyfish exhibits
- **Time**: 2-3 hours
- **Cost**: CAD 47 adult
- **Best for**: Families, rainy days

### St. Lawrence Market
- **What**: Historic market since 1803
- **Why go**: Peameal bacon sandwich, local vendors, Saturday farmers market
- **Time**: 1-2 hours
- **Cost**: Free entry, budget CAD 20-40 for food
- **When**: Saturday morning for farmers market (best time)

### Distillery District
- **What**: Pedestrian-only historic district, Victorian industrial architecture
- **Why go**: Galleries, boutiques, restaurants, no cars
- **Time**: 2-4 hours
- **Cost**: Free to walk, budget for food/drinks
- **Best for**: Evening stroll, brunch, photography

### Toronto Islands
- **What**: Chain of islands 15 min ferry from downtown
- **Why go**: Beaches, skyline views, car-free escape
- **Time**: Half day minimum
- **Cost**: CAD 9 ferry round trip
- **Best for**: Summer days, cycling, families

### Royal Ontario Museum (ROM)
- **What**: Canada's largest museum of world cultures and natural history
- **Why go**: Dinosaurs, gems, ancient civilizations, Michael Lee-Chin Crystal architecture
- **Time**: 3-4 hours
- **Cost**: CAD 26 adult
- **Skip if**: Not into museums

### Kensington Market
- **What**: Bohemian neighborhood with vintage shops, food stalls
- **Why go**: Eclectic vibe, diverse food, street art, local culture
- **Time**: 2-3 hours
- **Cost**: Free to walk
- **Best for**: Foodies, vintage shopping, counterculture

## Worth Visiting

### Art Gallery of Ontario (AGO)
- **What**: Major art museum, Frank Gehry renovation
- **Why go**: Canadian art (Group of Seven), European masters
- **Time**: 2-3 hours
- **Cost**: CAD 25, free Wed 6-9pm
- **Skip if**: Art not your thing

### Casa Loma
- **What**: Gothic Revival castle built 1914
- **Why go**: Architecture, gardens, escape rooms, filming location
- **Time**: 2-3 hours
- **Cost**: CAD 40 adult
- **Skip if**: Not into historic houses

### Hockey Hall of Fame
- **What**: Shrine to hockey history
- **Why go**: Stanley Cup, interactive exhibits, Canadian culture
- **Time**: 2-3 hours
- **Cost**: CAD 25 adult
- **Must for**: Sports fans, understanding Canadian culture

### High Park
- **What**: 400-acre urban park in west end
- **Why go**: Cherry blossoms (late April), zoo (free), hiking trails
- **Time**: 2-4 hours
- **Cost**: Free
- **Best for**: Nature lovers, cherry blossom season

### Graffiti Alley
- **What**: Legal street art corridor in Queen West
- **Why go**: Instagram photos, rotating murals
- **Time**: 30 min - 1 hour
- **Cost**: Free
- **Location**: Rush Lane between Portland and Spadina

## Skip or Lower Priority

### Yonge-Dundas Square
- **Reality**: Toronto's Times Square attempt, mostly ads
- **Alternative**: Walk through but don't plan time here

### Ripley's Believe It or Not
- **Reality**: Tourist trap, low value
- **Alternative**: Skip entirely

### Medieval Times
- **Reality**: Overpriced dinner theater
- **Alternative**: Skip unless with kids who love knights

### Toronto Zoo
- **Reality**: Very far from downtown (1+ hour), huge walking
- **Alternative**: Go only if you have full day and car, or Ripley's Aquarium

### Canada's Wonderland
- **Reality**: Theme park 45 min north, full day commitment
- **Alternative**: Only if you specifically want roller coasters and have a car

## Seasonal Highlights

### Spring (Apr-May)
- Cherry blossoms at High Park (late April)
- Doors Open Toronto (free building tours)

### Summer (Jun-Aug)
- Toronto Islands beaches
- Outdoor concerts at Budweiser Stage
- Pride Month (June) - one of world's largest
- Caribana (August) - Caribbean carnival
- CNE (Canadian National Exhibition) - late August

### Fall (Sep-Nov)
- TIFF (Toronto International Film Festival) - September
- Fall colors at Don Valley, High Park
- Nuit Blanche - overnight art festival (October)

### Winter (Dec-Mar)
- Nathan Phillips Square skating
- Distillery District Christmas Market
- Cavalcade of Lights
- PATH underground city exploration

# East Toronto Neighborhoods

## Leslieville

### Overview
Brunch capital of Toronto. Former industrial area now home to young families, foodies, and coffee obsessives.

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 2,000-2,600
- **Vibe**: Foodie, family-friendly, community
- **Transit**: Queen streetcar (501), King streetcar
- **Walk Score**: 89

### Pros
- Best brunch scene in city
- Great coffee shops
- Family-friendly
- Community feel
- More affordable than downtown

### Cons
- Streetcar commute slow
- Limited nightlife
- Gentrifying (less affordable each year)
- Far from subway

### Best For
- Foodies
- Young families
- Remote workers
- Those who hate club scenes

### Key Spots
- Lady Marmalade (brunch)
- Skin & Bones (coffee)
- Te Aro (coffee)
- Queen Street East strip

## Riverside / South Riverdale

### Overview
Just east of downtown, historic Broadview Hotel, evolving rapidly.

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 2,100-2,700
- **Vibe**: Historic, trendy, evolving
- **Transit**: Broadview station, Queen streetcar
- **Walk Score**: 92

### Pros
- Broadview Hotel (rooftop)
- Close to downtown
- Character streets
- Good transit (subway accessible)

### Cons
- Still developing
- Transitional in parts
- Limited grocery

### Best For
- Those wanting downtown adjacency
- Broadview Hotel fans
- Community seekers

### Key Spots
- Broadview Hotel
- Queen East shops
- Riverside Common

## Beaches (The Beach)

### Overview
Beach-town-in-the-city. Boardwalk, volleyball, year-round community. Locals debate "Beaches" vs "The Beach."

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 2,000-2,500
- **Vibe**: Beach community, family, laid-back
- **Transit**: Queen streetcar (long ride), some buses
- **Walk Score**: 87

### Pros
- Boardwalk (2+ km)
- Beach volleyball
- Small-town feel
- Safe, family-friendly
- Year-round community events

### Cons
- Far from downtown (40+ min transit)
- Limited nightlife
- Insular community
- Cold beach winters

### Best For
- Beach lovers
- Families
- Joggers/cyclists
- Those okay with long commute

### Key Spots
- Queen Street East shops
- Woodbine Beach
- Kew Gardens
- Fox Theatre

---

## Danforth / Greektown

### Overview
Greek heritage, Taste of the Danforth festival, great value. Connected by Bloor-Danforth subway.

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 1,700-2,300
- **Vibe**: Greek heritage, diverse, value
- **Transit**: Pape, Donlands, Greenwood stations
- **Walk Score**: 92

### Pros
- Subway access
- Affordable
- Great Greek food
- Family-friendly
- Community events

### Cons
- Less trendy than west end
- Some retail gaps
- Quieter at night
- Patchy development

### Best For
- Budget-conscious with transit needs
- Families
- Greek food lovers
- Those who want space + subway

### Key Spots
- Greek restaurants on Danforth
- Taste of the Danforth (August festival)
- Carrot Common
- Withrow Park

## East York / Broadview North

### Overview
Often overlooked, but affordable and improving. Historic homes, parks, quiet residential.

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 1,600-2,100
- **Vibe**: Residential, quiet, value
- **Transit**: Bus to Broadview/Pape stations
- **Walk Score**: 75

### Pros
- Affordable
- Larger spaces
- Parks (Taylor Creek)
- Character homes

### Cons
- Bus-dependent
- Less walkable
- Fewer amenities
- Quiet (boring?)

### Best For
- Families seeking space
- Budget-conscious
- Nature lovers
- Car owners

## Cabbagetown

### Overview
Victorian homes, gayborhood heritage, central but quieter. Named for Irish immigrants' cabbage gardens.

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 1,900-2,500
- **Vibe**: Historic, established, LGBTQ+ friendly
- **Transit**: Near Castle Frank station, Carlton streetcar
- **Walk Score**: 90

### Pros
- Beautiful Victorian architecture
- Near downtown
- Riverdale Park
- LGBTQ+ welcoming
- Character

### Cons
- Transitional in parts
- Some rougher blocks
- Limited retail

### Best For
- Those who love Victorian homes
- LGBTQ+ community
- Park lovers
- Architecture buffs

### Key Spots
- Parliament Street
- Riverdale Farm
- Riverdale Park (skyline views)
- Allan Gardens

---

## Regent Park

### Overview
Massive revitalization. Former social housing now mixed-income. Aquatic centre, new condos, community focus.

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 1,700-2,200
- **Vibe**: Transforming, diverse, mixed-income
- **Transit**: Near Dundas, walking to downtown
- **Walk Score**: 93

### Pros
- Affordable for location
- New amenities (Aquatic Centre)
- Diverse community
- Near downtown
- Community-focused development

### Cons
- Stigma (outdated)
- Still evolving
- Limited retail
- Transitional feel

### Best For
- Budget-conscious downtown seekers
- Those open to evolving neighborhoods
- Community-minded people

### Key Spots
- Regent Park Aquatic Centre
- Daniels Spectrum (arts)
- New grocery stores arriving

---

## Don Valley / Todmorden

### Overview
Nature corridor in the city. Don Valley trails, hidden village feel.

### Stats
- **Rent (1BR)**: CAD 1,800-2,400
- **Vibe**: Nature, residential, hidden
- **Transit**: Limited (Broadview, bus)
- **Walk Score**: 65

### Pros
- Don Valley ravine access
- Nature trails
- Quiet, private
- Hidden gem feel

### Cons
- Poor transit
- Isolated
- Car-dependent
- Flooding risk in valley

### Best For
- Nature lovers
- Cyclists/runners
- Those with cars
- Privacy seekers

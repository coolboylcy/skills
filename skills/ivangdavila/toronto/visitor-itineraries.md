# Toronto Itineraries

## 1-Day Itinerary (First Timer)

### Morning (9am-12pm)
1. **St. Lawrence Market** (9-10:30am)
   - Peameal bacon sandwich at Carousel Bakery
   - Browse vendors, get coffee

2. **Walk to Distillery District** (10:30-12pm)
   - 15 min walk east
   - Explore Victorian architecture
   - Coffee at Balzac's

### Afternoon (12pm-5pm)
3. **CN Tower** (12-2pm)
   - Skip lines: book timed entry online
   - Include glass floor if not afraid of heights
   - Lunch at 360 Restaurant (rotates, pricey but includes admission)

4. **Ripley's Aquarium OR ROM** (2:30-5pm)
   - Aquarium: at CN Tower base, great for all ages
   - ROM: take subway to Museum station, better for culture/history

### Evening (5pm-10pm)
5. **Kensington Market** (5-7pm)
   - Walk or streetcar west
   - Explore vintage shops, street art
   - Early dinner at one of many options

6. **Queen West** (7-10pm)
   - Walk down Queen Street
   - Graffiti Alley (Rush Lane)
   - Drinks at a rooftop bar

## 3-Day Itinerary (Comprehensive)

### Day 1: Downtown Core

**Morning**
- St. Lawrence Market (breakfast)
- Distillery District

**Afternoon**
- CN Tower + Ripley's Aquarium
- Walk along Harbourfront

**Evening**
- Dinner in Entertainment District (King West)
- Catch a show (Mirvish theatre, Second City)

### Day 2: Culture & Neighborhoods

**Morning**
- ROM (open 10am)
- Walk through Yorkville (upscale shopping, galleries)

**Afternoon**
- Lunch in Yorkville or The Annex
- Walk down Bloor to University
- Art Gallery of Ontario

**Evening**
- Kensington Market exploration
- Dinner in Chinatown (Spadina)
- Drinks in Queen West

### Day 3: Islands & East End

**Morning**
- Ferry to Toronto Islands (Jack Layton Terminal)
- Rent bikes, explore Centre Island
- Beach time (summer)

**Afternoon**
- Return to city, lunch at Leslieville
- Walk along Queen East
- Explore Riverside neighborhood

**Evening**
- Dinner in Leslieville or Riverside
- Sunset at Sugar Beach or Polson Pier

## Rainy Day Options

1. **PATH Underground City**
   - 30+ km of underground shopping
   - Connect Union Station to Eaton Centre

2. **Museums**
   - ROM, AGO, Bata Shoe Museum, Gardiner Museum

3. **Entertainment**
   - Escape rooms (multiple downtown)
   - Board game cafes (Snakes & Lattes)
   - Movies at TIFF Bell Lightbox

4. **Food Halls**
   - Assembly Chef's Hall
   - St. Lawrence Market (covered)

## Budget-Conscious Tips

### Free Activities
- Walk Distillery District
- Kensington Market and Chinatown
- Graffiti Alley
- High Park (including free zoo)
- Beaches (Woodbine, Bluffs)
- AGO free Wednesday 6-9pm
- Trinity Bellwoods park people-watching

### Money-Saving
- CityPASS for major attractions (if doing 4+)
- Presto card for transit (cheaper than tokens)
- Lunch specials instead of dinner
- Grocery store prepared foods
- BYOB at some restaurants (check policy)

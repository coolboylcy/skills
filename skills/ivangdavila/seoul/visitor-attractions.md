# Seoul Attractions — Must-See vs Skip

## Must-See (Absolutely Worth It)

### Gyeongbokgung Palace (경복궁)
- **Why**: Largest and most iconic of the Five Grand Palaces
- **Time**: 2-3 hours minimum
- **Pro tip**: Wear hanbok (free rental nearby) for free entry and photos
- **Best time**: Opening ceremony at 10 AM, guard changing at 10 AM and 2 PM
- **Skip**: Weekends — extremely crowded

### Bukchon Hanok Village (북촌한옥마을)
- **Why**: 600-year-old traditional houses, still inhabited
- **Time**: 2-3 hours wandering
- **Pro tip**: Go early morning (before 9 AM) for empty streets and photos
- **Caution**: Be quiet — residents live here. Signs ask for silence.

### Namsan Tower / N Seoul Tower (남산타워)
- **Why**: Best 360° view of Seoul
- **Time**: 1-2 hours
- **Pro tip**: Take cable car up, walk down through the forest
- **Best time**: Sunset — watch city lights come on

### Hongdae (홍대)
- **Why**: Youth culture, street performers, nightlife, indie music
- **Time**: Evening onwards (after 6 PM)
- **Pro tip**: Friday/Saturday nights have the best street performances
- **Skip**: Daytime — just another shopping area

### Myeongdong (명동)
- **Why**: K-beauty shopping mecca, street food
- **Time**: 3-4 hours
- **Pro tip**: Go for shopping AND street food. Sample everything.
- **Skip**: If you don't care about skincare/cosmetics

### Changdeokgung Palace + Secret Garden (창덕궁)
- **Why**: Most beautiful palace, UNESCO World Heritage
- **Time**: 2-3 hours (Secret Garden requires guided tour)
- **Pro tip**: Book Secret Garden tour in advance (limited spots)

### Gwangjang Market (광장시장)
- **Why**: Oldest traditional market, best bindaetteok, mayak kimbap
- **Time**: 1-2 hours
- **Pro tip**: Go hungry. Eat at the stalls, not restaurants.
- **Best time**: Lunch or early dinner

### Lotte World Tower Observatory (롯데월드타워)
- **Why**: Tallest building in Korea, glass floor at 500m
- **Time**: 1-2 hours
- **Pro tip**: Book sunset time slot, includes night views
- **Skip**: If you've been to Namsan Tower and budget is tight

### DMZ (Demilitarized Zone)
- **Why**: Unique historical site, glimpse into North Korea
- **Time**: Full day tour required
- **Pro tip**: Book USO or Koridoor tours (best access)
- **Note**: Bring passport. Dress code enforced.

## Worth It If Time Allows

### Insadong (인사동)
- Traditional arts, antiques, tea houses
- Good for: Souvenirs, galleries, traditional tea
- Time: 2-3 hours

### Itaewon
- International food, diverse nightlife, LGBTQ+-friendly area
- Good for: Western food cravings, diverse crowd
- Time: Evening onwards

### War Memorial of Korea
- Extensive Korean War history, free entry
- Good for: History buffs, families
- Time: 2-4 hours

### COEX Mall + Starfield Library
- Massive underground mall, Instagram-famous library
- Good for: Rainy days, shopping, photo ops
- Time: 2-3 hours

### Jamsil (Lotte World)
- Indoor/outdoor theme park
- Good for: Families, K-drama fans (filming location)
- Time: Full day

### Noryangjin Fish Market
- Wholesale fish market, pick and eat
- Good for: Seafood lovers, authentic experience
- Time: 2-3 hours (go for lunch)

## Tourist Traps — Skip Unless Specific Interest

### Gangnam Style Statue
- **Reality**: Underwhelming photo op in a random plaza
- **Alternative**: Just walk around Gangnam for the vibe

### Line Friends Store (multiple locations)
- **Reality**: Overpriced character merchandise
- **Alternative**: Photos outside are free

### Trick Eye Museum
- **Reality**: Generic 3D illusion museum, nothing Korean-specific
- **Alternative**: Leeum Museum for actual art

### Generic "Traditional Village" performances
- **Reality**: Touristy, staged performances
- **Alternative**: Free street performances in Hongdae

### Lotte World (for solo adults)
- **Reality**: Long lines, better theme parks elsewhere
- **When to go**: With kids, or big K-drama/K-pop fan

## Seasonal Must-Sees

### Spring (April-May)
- **Yeouido Cherry Blossom Festival** — Pink tunnels of cherry blossoms
- **Changgyeonggung Night Opening** — Palace lit up at night

### Summer (June-August)
- **Han River Parks** — Evening picnics, chicken and beer
- **Boryeong Mud Festival** — Day trip to beach festival

### Fall (September-November)
- **Namsan Tower** — Fall foliage views
- **Nami Island** — Peak autumn colors, famous from K-dramas

### Winter (December-February)
- **Myeongdong Christmas lights** — Festive shopping
- **Ice skating at COEX** — Indoor rink
- **Ski resorts** — Day trips to Yongpyong, Phoenix Park

## Neighborhood Deep Dives

### Gwangjang Market (광장시장)

**Getting there**: Line 1, Jongno 5-ga Station Exit 8

**Must-try stalls**:
1. **Bindaetteok** (mung bean pancake) — Look for the busy ajumma
2. **Mayak kimbap** — "Drug" kimbap (addictively good mini rolls)
3. **Yukhoe** — Raw beef tartare
4. **Tteokbokki** — Vintage-style
5. **Soju + anju** — Pick any stall

**Tips**:
- Go at lunch or early evening
- Point at what looks good
- Share tables with strangers (normal)
- Cash preferred at some stalls

### Jongno 3-ga (종로 3가)

**The vibe**: Working-class, slightly rough, very authentic Seoul

**Famous for**:
- **Pojangmacha alleys** — Tent bars with grilled fish, soju
- **Nagwon-dong** — Musicians' alley, instrument shops
- **24-hour restaurants** — Seolleongtang, gukbap

**Best spots**:
- **골목집** (Golmokjip) — Classic pojangmacha vibe
- Any place packed with Korean salarymen

### Myeongdong (명동)

**Reality check**: Very touristy, but street food is legitimately good

**Worth it**:
- Street food walk (especially evening)
- Quick K-beauty shopping breaks
- Chain restaurants (consistent quality)

**Skip**:
- Overpriced sit-down restaurants
- "Authentic Korean BBQ" targeting tourists

**Best street foods here**:
- Egg bread (계란빵)
- Tornado potato (회오리감자)
- Hotteok
- Giant squid grilled whole

### Hongdae (홍대)

**Character**: Young, trendy, Instagram-friendly

**Food scenes**:
- **Yeonnam-dong** — Hipster cafés, brunch
- **Main Hongdae** — Fast food, chains, late-night
- **Sangsu** — Chill restaurants, wine bars

**Best for**:
- Group dinners before clubbing
- Café hopping
- Budget Korean food
- Fusion experiments

### Itaewon (이태원)

**Reality**: Best international food in Korea, period

**Sub-areas**:
- **Main street** — Mix of everything
- **HBC (Haebangchon)** — Craft beer, indie restaurants
- **Gyeongnidan-gil** — Upscale international
- **Usadan-ro** — Emerging, trendy

**What to eat**:
- BBQ (American, not Korean)
- Middle Eastern
- Mexican
- Indian
- Brunch

### Gangnam/Cheongdam (강남/청담)

**Character**: Expensive, business-oriented, special occasions

**Best for**:
- Michelin-starred restaurants
- Business dinners
- Premium Korean BBQ
- Designer food experiences

**Tips**:
- Reservations essential for upscale spots
- Dress code: smart casual minimum
- Expense account territory

### Seongsu (성수)

**Character**: Brooklyn vibes, converted warehouses, hipster heaven

**Famous for**:
- **Café Onion** — Industrial bakery in old factory
- Concept stores with attached cafés
- Instagrammable everything

**Food scene**:
- Brunch spots
- Coffee roasters
- Craft bakeries
- New restaurant openings weekly

## Food Tours

### Self-Guided Routes

**Route 1: Traditional (4 hours)**
1. Tongin Market (dosirak with coins)
2. Walk to Seochon (café break)
3. Gwangjang Market (bindaetteok, soju)
4. Walk to Jongno 3-ga (pojangmacha finish)

**Route 2: Modern (3 hours)**
1. Seongsu (Café Onion)
2. Metro to Itaewon
3. HBC walk (Magpie Brewing)
4. Gyeongnidan-gil dinner

**Route 3: Night Food (4 hours)**
1. Korean BBQ dinner (anywhere)
2. Walk it off
3. Chimaek (chicken + beer)
4. Pojangmacha nightcap
5. Convenience store ramyeon (2 AM)

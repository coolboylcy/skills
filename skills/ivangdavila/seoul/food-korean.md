# Korean Cuisine Essentials

## BBQ (고기)

### Pork (돼지고기)

| Cut | Korean | Notes |
|-----|--------|-------|
| **Pork belly** | 삼겹살 (samgyeopsal) | Most popular, fatty |
| **Pork neck** | 목살 (moksal) | Leaner, flavorful |
| **Pork jowl** | 항정살 (hangjeongsal) | Premium, chewy |
| **Pork shoulder** | 앞다리살 (apdarisal) | Budget option |
| **Marinated pork** | 양념삼겹살 | Sweet/spicy marinade |

### Beef (소고기)

| Cut | Korean | Notes |
|-----|--------|-------|
| **Short ribs** | 갈비 (galbi) | Classic, bone-in |
| **Ribeye** | 꽃등심 (kkotdeungsim) | Premium, marbled |
| **Sirloin** | 등심 (deungsim) | Lean, tender |
| **Brisket** | 차돌박이 (chadolbagi) | Thin-sliced, quick cook |
| **Bulgogi** | 불고기 | Marinated, sweet |
| **Korean beef** | 한우 (hanwoo) | Premium Korean cattle, expensive |

### How to BBQ
1. Grill meat until edges brown
2. Cut with scissors into bite-sized pieces
3. Wrap in lettuce (상추) with:
   - Grilled garlic (마늘)
   - Ssamjang (쌈장) — thick paste
   - Sliced raw garlic
   - Grilled kimchi (optional)
4. Eat in one bite (쌈)
5. Chase with soju shot (optional but encouraged)

### Where to Go
- **Budget**: Any 고기집 near universities
- **Quality**: Majang-dong (meat market district)
- **Premium**: Maple Tree House, Byeokje Galbi

## Soups & Stews (국/찌개)

### Jjigae (찌개) — Thick Stews

| Dish | What's In It | Spice Level |
|------|--------------|-------------|
| **김치찌개** (Kimchi-jjigae) | Aged kimchi, pork, tofu | Medium-hot |
| **된장찌개** (Doenjang-jjigae) | Fermented soybean paste, vegetables | Mild |
| **순두부찌개** (Sundubu-jjigae) | Soft tofu, seafood/pork, egg | Medium-hot |
| **부대찌개** (Budae-jjigae) | "Army stew" — spam, sausage, ramyeon | Medium |
| **청국장** (Cheonggukjang) | Strong fermented soybean | Mild (but pungent) |

### Guk (국) — Lighter Soups

| Dish | What's In It |
|------|--------------|
| **미역국** (Miyeok-guk) | Seaweed soup (birthday tradition) |
| **갈비탕** (Galbitang) | Short rib soup |
| **설렁탕** (Seolleongtang) | Ox bone soup (white, milky) |
| **삼계탕** (Samgyetang) | Ginseng chicken soup |
| **해장국** (Haejangguk) | Hangover soup (various styles) |

### Where to Go
- **Kimchi-jjigae**: Ojang-dong Hamheung Naengmyeon (yes, they have both)
- **Samgyetang**: Tosokchon (near Gyeongbokgung)
- **Seolleongtang**: Imun Seolleongtang (oldest in Seoul)

## Fried Chicken (치킨)

### A National Obsession

Fried chicken + beer = 치맥 (chimaek). Korea's unofficial national dish combination.

### Styles

| Style | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **후라이드** (Fried) | Classic crispy |
| **양념** (Yangnyeom) | Sweet-spicy glazed |
| **간장** (Ganjang) | Soy garlic glazed |
| **파닭** (Padak) | Topped with green onions |
| **반반** (Banban) | Half and half (two styles) |
| **뿌링클** (Bburinkle) | Cheese powder coated (BHC) |

### Top Chains
1. **BBQ Chicken** — Original gangster
2. **Kyochon** — Crispy, consistent
3. **BHC** — Creative flavors
4. **Pelicana** — Old-school classic
5. **Nene** — Good value
6. **Goobne** — Oven-roasted (healthier?)

### Delivery
- Order via 배달의민족 (Baedal) or Coupang Eats
- Minimum usually ₩15,000-20,000
- Delivery fee ₩0-3,000
- Arrives in 30-40 minutes
